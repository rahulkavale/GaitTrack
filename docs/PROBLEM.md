# The Problem

## Who this is for

Parents and physiotherapists working with children who have cerebral palsy (CP) or similar motor disorders affecting gait and mobility.

## The core problem

A child with cerebral palsy undergoes months and years of physiotherapy to improve their walking. But today, there is no affordable, accessible way to **objectively measure** whether therapy is working.

### What happens now

- A physiotherapist observes the child walking and makes subjective assessments: "his left knee seems better this week"
- Progress is tracked through clinical notes and memory
- Parents are told "he's improving" but can't see concrete evidence
- Small regressions can go unnoticed for weeks
- When the child sees a new therapist, there's no transferable data — just notes
- Clinical gait analysis labs exist but cost thousands of dollars per visit, require hospital appointments, and are not practical for regular monitoring

### Why this matters

- Cerebral palsy affects approximately 1 in 500 children worldwide
- Early intervention and consistent therapy are critical — the brain's plasticity is highest in the first few years
- Without objective measurement, therapy adjustments are reactive rather than proactive
- Parents carry enormous emotional burden and uncertainty: "Is this working? Are we doing enough?"
- Therapists make treatment decisions based on limited, subjective data

## The gap

| Clinical gait labs | Our app |
|---|---|
| $2,000-5,000 per visit | Free |
| Requires hospital appointment | Works on any phone |
| Done once every few months | Can be used at every session |
| Requires trained technician | Anyone can operate it |
| Report takes weeks | Instant results |
| Not sharable in real-time | Shared between parents and therapists |

The gap is not in the quality of analysis — clinical labs will always be more precise. The gap is in **accessibility and frequency**. A slightly less precise measurement done 50 times is more valuable than a perfect measurement done once.

## What our app does

Gait Tracker uses the phone's camera and Google's MediaPipe pose estimation (running entirely in the browser) to:

1. **Detect body landmarks** in real-time as the child walks
2. **Draw a stick figure overlay** so parents and therapists can see what the model sees
3. **Calculate gait metrics**: joint angles (knee, hip, ankle), bilateral symmetry, stride cadence, step count
4. **Support multiple camera angles** in a single session for comprehensive analysis
5. **Track progress over time** with charts showing trends across days, weeks, and months
6. **Share data** between parents, therapists, and care teams

## How it helps real users

### For parents
- See concrete numbers instead of vague reassurances
- Track progress between therapy sessions
- Record sessions at home during daily activities
- Share data with remote therapists or specialists
- Build confidence that therapy is making a difference (or flag when it's not)

### For physiotherapists
- Objective baseline measurements from day one
- Data-driven therapy adjustments
- Track multiple patients' progress over time
- Receive recordings from parents between sessions
- Better communication with parents using actual data

### For the child
- More targeted therapy based on real measurements
- Earlier detection of regressions
- Better outcomes through data-informed treatment decisions
